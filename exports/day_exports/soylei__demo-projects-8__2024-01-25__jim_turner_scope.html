<@U06F97BPQJU> A more general question for you, somewhat related to the above discussion: what do you think our level of interest on derivative molecules of the Rejuv platform?Expanding on the idea talked about in the <#C06F7SD9Z0A> channel, one of the ideas is intentionally reaction the epoxy with something to change properties. This can be things like organic acids, epoxy curatives (amines/thiols), etc. We've had concerns about that causing regulatory hurdles associated with that approach and I'm hoping you have some thoughts. To the flame retardant idea, if we wanted to run with it and combine compatibility with shingles and similar material with flame retardants, we could look at grafting actual flame retardants onto the epoxies. For something like that we would likely be able to use commodity ESO, rather than SESO, which is a lot more commercially available. For example, throw ESO/SESO in a pot with something like chlorendic acid and cook it for a bit and you'll have a flame-retardant veg-oil. Chlorendic acid is probably not the best choice because of politics of halogenated flame retardants, but I used it as an example because it's the first thing that came up and has the necessary functional groups. If this idea is of interest, we can expand it out a bit, but I'd like to hear your thoughts on regulations first.
