<@U06F97BPQJU> We've worked with Danimer a couple of times at ISU through the CB2 connection. I don't know how far into the weeds you dug into them. They are primarily a PHA company. PHA's are cool bioplastics that Eric and I have both worked with a fair bit over the past 5 years. They are neat because you can tweak the biochemistry to really adjust properties. There are amorphous PHA's, highly crystalline PHA's, flexible and weak, brittle and strong, and lots in between. If you were to ask my opinion on it, where PHA's fail is on their scalability. You're lucky to get a few grams out of every 100 mL of fermentation solution, and getting the plastic isolated from the bugs is not a trivial process either. Unless the titers go up by about 5-10x and a lot of other things fall into place, these are likely to stay low-volume/high-value niche application plastics. That said, if your point is to draw attention to how good companies like Danimer are at marketing, yeah I think there are probably things we can learn from them.
